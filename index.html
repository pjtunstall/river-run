Where Does it Flow From?

It's been a zigzag path to coding. I've sailed schooners on the Great Lakes and tended sheep in snowy Cumbria. I've studied Old Norse and Mathematics. I wrote a book. Is there a pattern here?

riverrun, past Eve and Adams, from swerve of shore to bend of bay

In 2022, I joined 01Founders, a peer-led, project-based bootcamp-style course. It quickly became my absolute consuming passion. I now have three years' experience of Go, two and a half of JavaScript, and one of Rust.

It's an easy, gentle-flowing river.

I've built what I hope is a solid foundation in software development through complex solo and group projects. I've loved the camaraderie of the course. It's highly collaborative.

Away, you rolling river.

I'm drawn to coding because I find it hard to resist a good puzzle. I love how there's always more to learn, whichever way you look, however deep you go. Web development is especially fun because you get to affect a user's experience directly, and the results are accessible to anyone with an internet connnection.

Away, we're bound away, across the wide Missouri.

I'm now seeking my first professional role as a developer. I feel very ready for the next challenge. I can offer curiosity, reliability, and a strong work ethic. I'm looking for a supportive team, some mentorship, and a chance to show what I can do.

Don't let the whimsy fool you. I'm quite set on this.
